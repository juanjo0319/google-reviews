## FAQ

### How long should a negative review response be?

**Aim for 50-150 words.** Anything shorter feels dismissive. Anything longer feels defensive. Your response should acknowledge the issue, show empathy, describe what you've done, and offer an offline path. That's it. Resist the urge to over-explain.

### Should I offer a discount or freebie in my response?

Not publicly, in most cases. Publicly offering compensation for every negative review trains customers to complain for discounts. Instead, **move the conversation offline first**, then offer compensation privately. The exception is clear-cut service failures where a gesture of goodwill is obviously appropriate.

### What if the negative review is completely false?

Don't accuse the reviewer of lying. Stay professional, note that you can't find a matching record, and ask them to contact you with details. **If the review violates Google's policies**, flag it for removal through Google Business Profile. Your calm, professional public response does more for your reputation than the false review damages it.
